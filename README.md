# airbnb-data-analysis
<h2>Overview</h2>
An interactive two-page Power BI dashboard analyzing global hotel listings, property rental trends, and customer satisfaction across 279,712 hotel listings in 10 major cities using a global Airbnb performance dataset. This project uncovers year-over-year market patterns, pricing behavior, occupancy trends, and guest experience insights to support data-driven decisions for property rental companies, real estate advisors, and hospitality businesses operating in the global market.

<h2>Key Business Insights</h2>
<p><b>Global Airbnb Market Snapshot:</b>The average Airbnb listing generates strong demand in high-tourism cities, but performance varies sharply by location, with a small group of cities contributing the majority of bookings, revenue, and guest engagement across the global market.</p>

<p><b>Global Listing Dominance:</b> Paris, London, and New York consistently dominate Airbnb’s global listing ecosystem, reinforcing that Airbnb demand is heavily concentrated in tourism-first metropolitan markets with strong international traveler inflow.</p>
<p><b>Property Type Preference:</b> Entire homes and apartments remain the most commonly listed Airbnb property types across global cities, significantly outperforming private and shared rooms, showing clear traveler preference for privacy, convenience, and full-property access.</p>

<p><b>Customer Experience Drives Profitability:</b> Listings with consistently higher guest ratings and review volumes show stronger occupancy and pricing power, proving that customer satisfaction is one of the strongest drivers of long-term rental profitability.</p>

<p><b>Market Concentration Risk:</b> A large share of Airbnb revenue is concentrated in a limited number of high-performing cities and property types, increasing market dependency and highlighting the need for geographic and portfolio diversification.</p>
